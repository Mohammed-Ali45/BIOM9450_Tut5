Event Schedule

Find all the major talks, presentations, workshops and their details across the 3 days the event runs here.

The entire workshop will be held on the UNSW Campus in Kensington, 2033. On all 3 days, the roundhouse (lower campus) will be open as a hub for mingling as well as a venue for the larger events. A map of the campus can be found below, and a digital copy with labels and legends for individual buildings can be found here

Bathrooms are located in each of the buildings hosting events as well as most other buildings. The workshop will start at 9am in the Roundhouse on Friday 17th November.

For any issues or inquiries during the workshop, please call us at 9584 3847

- Day 1: Friday 17th November
- Day 2: Saturday 18th November

Friday 17th November

11am-1pm

What are the motivations for rigorous EHR?


What exactly is the point of being so nitpicky about the way we store info about patients? Why do we need standards for classification of diseases or therapies? Are medical textbooks not enough? Why is a task as simple as taking vitals contingent on so many complex systems? What are those systems comprised of? Come see experts in the field of Health coding paint the big picture that will set the stage for the rest of the weekend

John Clancy Auditorium, Upper Campus
2pm-3pm

Health software development workshop


What the hell is an ISO 11073? How can I integrate my health software into the greater digital health infrastructure? Engineers from the International Standards Organisation and Continua provide the skeleton for a well implemented health software

K-H6-G16, Tyree Energy Technology

The end of the X-ray View Box?


A masterclass in imaging messaging standards, DICOM outlines the steps necessary to establish themselves as the leaders in Digital communications and imaging in medicine.

LG02 Medical Sciences Seminar Room, C27 Wallace Wurth
4pm-6pm

ICD-10-AM Focus session


Australia has implemented the International Classification of Diseases and Health Problems, Tenth Revision, with an Australian Modification. Staff legislators from the NSW Health Ministry will be joined by practitioners from the nearby Prince Of Wales Hospital where they will give the insider flick on why it is important to have these standards in place for hospitals private and public across Australia.

Saturday 18th November

11am-12pm

First Day Recap


We understand not everyone can be everywhere all at once. This short session will serve to summarise the amazing talks and keynotes that were given on the prior first day of the event, and provide guidance on accessing the associated resources.

Roundhouse, Lower Campus
12pm-1pm

SNOMED CT and it's recent update


SNOMED CT is the global standard for scientifically validated digitised medical terminology. It's importance can be as specific as ensuring two patients with similar sicknesses have equally similar EHRs, but can be as wide in scope as to have implications in epedemiology. Representatives talk about what's new and what's next.

M15 Rupert Myers, Upper Campus
3pm-4pm

COVID and its implications


The unprecedented pandemic that started in 2020 with the rapid spreading of the COVID-19 virus took much of the world by surprise, and shocked healthcare systems in Australia, with a massive uptick in TeleHealth. So what have we learned, and how might we equip digital infrastructure to better prepare ourselves for a similar pandemic in the future?

John Clancy Auditorium, Upper Campus
4pm-6pm

HL7 Focus session


The task of securing Electronic Health Records is one of utmost priority. The protection of patient confidentiality and the security of their health records goes hand in hand. But the representatives from HL7 will argue that the dedication to this end can and has been excessive. They will also discuss the importance of interoperability and accessibility where it is due and how patients stand to benefit from HL7's proliferation.

Sunday 19th November

10am-12pm

2nd day recap and research showcase


As on Saturday, there will be a short summary of the previous talks and a pointer towards useful resources from those talks.
The main event will involve the showcase of new research and business ideas by students and professionals alike. Posters and stalls will be laid out for attendees to consume and discuss with the authors.

Roundhouse, Lower Campus
1:30pm-3pm

Interview with WONZA about ICP-2


On standardising digital healthcare practices, the World Organisation of Family Doctors, WONZA is at the forefront when it comes to supplying healthcare providers with the necessary data to make decisions to refine primary care. There is also a need to classify data in a simpler, less granular manner than offered by the ICD standards. Representatives will, in detail, discuss the complex relationships between patients, primary care doctors, and healthcare providers at the government level and where they fit into the equation in an international context.

John Clancy Auditorium, Upper Campus

Skill workshop


Across several rooms in close proximity to the main hub in the roundhouse, there will be several skill based workshops working on developing competency in the ISO 11073 Standards, with a walkthrough of a hypothetical case of the design, manufacture, and deployment of an ECG machine, a device very often utilised in primary care medicine by various medical staff.

G05, G07, E8 Science and Engineering, Lower Campus
4pm-6pm

Wrap up and gift ceremony


At this point, there will have been a large number of talks and presentations in the many facets of digital healthcare infrastructure and where Australia and the world are headed in the near future. The greatest learnings will be compiled and presented in the context of the workshop as a whole.
There will also be gifts handed out to the presenters and teachers that form the backbone of the workshop and make it so valuable.
